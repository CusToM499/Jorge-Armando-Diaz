Barichara Tour

Barichara is a Colombian municipality located in the department of Santander. It is part of the province of Guanent?. The town is recognized for its buildings from the late 18th century; The old sector of the population was declared cultural heritage of the country by decree 1654 of 1978.4 This municipality is popularly known for being "the most beautiful town in Colombia", which is why it has been awarded the title of National Monument.5 It is part of the Network of heritage towns of Colombia.

Barichara today ?has gastronomy, hotels, the connection with extreme sports, the connection with ecotourism, and this beauty that we see here in the landscape that is a municipality that has the architectural development hand in hand with the protection of nature?6

Barichara is built on stone. Its streets, houses, temples and especially the cemetery, a place notable for its bars and windows, are built with yellow stone. The Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception in memory of the Virgin of the Rock is a unique work, it is supported by 10 carved monolithic columns 5 meters high and 70 cm wide.

The historic center of Barichara was declared national monument on August 3, 1978 taking into account that "it is a good example of informal urban development in the Andalusian way, and presents a good state of conservation of the original urban layout, the construction from the end of the 18th century and the environment itself of that time".
