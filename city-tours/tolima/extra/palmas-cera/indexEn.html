Categories: Cultural Tourism - Ecological Tourism - Fauna and Flora



Wax Palms

In good conditions, it grows up to 70 meters. It is the largest monocotyledon in the world. Its leaves are dark green and grayish, with petioles more than 2 m long. The trunk is cylindrical, smooth, and covered with wax. When the leaves die and fall, they leave a black ring around the stem. It is a protected species. Ceroxylon quindiuense has extremely tall growth, and lives for more than one hundred years. It was studied by Alexander von Humboldt in 1801. It is a plant in danger of extinction.
